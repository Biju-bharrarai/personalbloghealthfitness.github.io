Regualr Exercise

Exercise is essential for maintaining fitness, improving overall health, and assisting in the prevention of obesity, hypertension, and cardiovascular disease.According to surveys carried out by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), 61.5% of children between the ages of nine and thirteen do not engage in any form of organized physical activity, such as sports or dance classes, and 22.6% do not engage in physical activity in their free time.The American Obesity Association estimates that 15% of children and adolescents aged six to 19 are obese and 30% of children and adolescents aged six to 19 are overweight.

Yoga

Yoga is a spiritual, mental, and physical practice from ancient India.The practice was actually passed down from teacher to student long before this book was written, and the wise man Patanjali codified it in his Yoga Sutras around 400 C.E.This used to be done one-on-one, but group classes have become the norm since yoga became popular in the West in the 20th century. The Sanskrit root yuj, which means "to yoke" or "to unite," is where the word "yoga" gets its name. The practice aims to unite the individual self and universal consciousness as well as the mind, body, and spirit. A feeling of spiritual awakening is brought about by this union, which tends to neutralize ego-driven thoughts and actions.
